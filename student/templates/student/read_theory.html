Core Concepts of Cryptography

Cryptography is the process of protecting information by converting it into a secure format. It helps keep communication private and safe from unauthorized access.

In cryptography, the original readable message is called plaintext. After applying an encryption method, it becomes ciphertext, which cannot be understood easily without the correct key.

Encryption is the process of converting plaintext into ciphertext. Decryption is the reverse process, where ciphertext is converted back into readable plaintext using the correct method or key.

There are two major types of cryptography: symmetric cryptography and asymmetric cryptography. In symmetric cryptography, the same key is used for both encryption and decryption. In asymmetric cryptography, two different keys are used: a public key and a private key.

Cryptography is used in many real-world systems such as online banking, secure websites, messaging applications, digital signatures, and authentication systems.

Understanding these basic concepts is important because they form the foundation for advanced security topics such as secure communication, hashing, authentication, and digital certificates.
